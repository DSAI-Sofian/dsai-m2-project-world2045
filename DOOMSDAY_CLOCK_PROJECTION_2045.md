
# Doomsday Clock Projection for 2045

## Background

The Doomsday Clock maintained by the Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists is a symbolic measure of global existential risk. The current setting (2024–2025) is **85 seconds to midnight**, the closest in history.

## Analytical Interpretation Using World2045 Model

The World2045 analytical framework integrates indicators from multiple domains:

- economic development
- governance stability
- climate vulnerability
- adaptation readiness
- conflict risk

Trajectory analysis suggests:

1. Structural development advantages remain persistent.
2. Only a small number of countries show strong upward mobility.
3. Climate vulnerability and conflict risks remain elevated in several regions.

## Projection Method

Rather than predicting precise geopolitical events, the model provides a structural outlook:

- Climate vulnerability improves slowly in many regions.
- Conflict incidence remains persistent but not globally escalating.
- Governance trajectories show mixed outcomes.

These combined signals suggest **global systemic risk remains high but does not accelerate dramatically**.

## 2045 Estimate

Based on the trajectory indicators, a plausible symbolic estimate is:

**70 seconds to midnight in 2045.**

This represents a modest deterioration relative to today’s 85 seconds, driven mainly by:

- continued climate stress
- uneven governance outcomes
- persistent regional conflicts

However, strong development trajectories in parts of Europe, Asia, and the Americas prevent a catastrophic acceleration toward midnight.

## Interpretation

The projection implies a world where:

- systemic risks remain extremely high
- technological and economic development offset some dangers
- global cooperation is uneven but not entirely absent

The Doomsday Clock in 2045 would therefore likely remain **within the same critical risk band as today**, symbolizing a fragile but not collapsed global order.
